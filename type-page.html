Typography

Axial

In axial design, everything is divided using a single line or axis. In my example I’ve made a strong line going at and angle through the centre. I then have the text aligned along and coming out from the axis. Following this axis gives the reader a clear path what to read.

Radial

Radial typography has the text protruding out from the center of a circle, my design does just that. The hollow circle it positioned on the left of the image, and the text is coming out directly straight from the center point.

Dilatational

Dilatational typography follows the text around the center point of a circle. My design has the headings and title text revolve in larger, bolder circles with the longer paragraphs split into smaller segments of circles to creating an easier, clearer reading experience.

Random

The text in random typography is positioned in ways that don’t follow and certain patterns or styles, as shown in my example the text can be in all different positions, orientations, sizes and font styles.

Grid

Grid typography design as the text structured in grid patterns, with each positioning of the text in my example a clear grid can be seen to form with the squares spaces that the text occupies have strict positioning and spacing between each of them.

Modular

Modular typography has text placed in different ‘modules’ where their position isn’t as strict as grid design, but the text is still clearly separated and can be easily differentiated between. My example has the larger bodies of text in larger blockier modules whereas the easier more digestible information is placed in circular modules leading to each other one for easier consumption of information.

Transitional

Transitional design follows a natural flow and movement. My text has a natural wavey flow from the top left to the bottom of the right of the image. All the text follows the same path at different points in the journey. The text leads from one piece to another giving the reader a clear path and flow when reading the text.

Bilateral

Similar to axial design, Bilateral has a distinct axis at its center point but instead of coming out from the axis, the text is aligned on top of that central axis. For the most part of my design, the text positioning gets narrower to reinforce the hierarchy of information and to lead the reader down to the next bit of text.
